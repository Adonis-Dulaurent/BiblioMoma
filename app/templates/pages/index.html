Presentation

BiblioMoMA is a web application designed to provide users with information and bibliographic references on the works and artists featured in the collections of the Museum of Modern Art (MoMA). This application utilizes datasets produced by MoMA itself, available on GitHub, and cross-references them with data from OpenAlex (an open bibliographic database) and WikiData, allowing for an in-depth exploration of sources and authors related to the artworks.

BiblioMoMA makes discovering artists and artworks easier thanks to an advanced search system and the ability for users to create an account and compile a personalized collection of bibliographic references.

User-friendly and visually engaging due to the integration of images into its layout, BiblioMoMA is designed to be accessible to all audiences, from middle school students to art history researchers. However, its purpose extends beyond individual visitors: the application also serves as an innovative model for collection enhancement, aimed at other cultural institutions, including museums, looking to enrich public access to their heritage.

The BiblioMoMA web application was developed by a young team of students from the TNAH (Digital Technologies Applied to History) Master’s program at the École nationale des Chartes, as part of their final-year project.
